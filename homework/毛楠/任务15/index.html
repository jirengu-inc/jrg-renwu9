J.R.R. Tolkien

An English writer, poet, philologist, and university professor.



Childhood

As a child, he was bitten by a large baboon spider in the garden, an event some think later echoed in his stories, although Tolkien admitted no actual memory of the event and no special hatred of spiders as an adult.



Youth

In his early teens, he was studying Latin and Anglo-Saxon. Interest in the language soon died away, but Mary and others, including Tolkien himself, invented a new and more complex language called Nevbosh.



Academic and writing career

His first civilian job after World War I was at the Oxford English Dictionary.In 1920, he took up a post as Reader in English Language at the University of Leeds, and became the youngest professor there.
